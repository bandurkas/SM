## 1. Market Structure (Foundations)
Market structure is the primary "map" of the market. It is fractal (patterns repeat across timeframes).

### 1.1. Swings & Confirmation
*   **Swing Pattern**: A 3-candle or 5-candle formation where the middle candle is the highest/lowest.
*   **Higher High (HH) / Higher Low (HL)**: Bullish structure.
*   **Lower High (LH) / Lower Low (LL)**: Bearish structure.
*   **Break of Structure (BOS)**: When price closes (with body) beyond a previous swing high/low.
*   **Confirmation**: A trend is only confirmed once a BOS occurs. A swing is confirmed when it generates a BOS of the opposite side or continues the trend.

### 1.2. Structure Types
*   **External Structure (Swing Structure)**: Main trend swings on HTF (High Time Frame).
*   **Internal Structure (Minor/Sub-structure)**: Price action within external swings.
    *   **Minor BOS (mBOS)**: Internal structure shifts, often used for early entries.
*   **Fractal Nature**: 1H structure consists of 15m mBOS and 5m sub-structures.

## 2. Liquidity (The Fuel)
Smart Money moves price to areas where retail orders (stop-losses) are concentrated.

### 2.1. Liquidity Pools
*   **Buy Stops Liquidity (BSL)**: Concentrated above highs (Old Highs, EQH). Smart Money sells here.
*   **Sell Stops Liquidity (SSL)**: Concentrated below lows (Old Lows, EQL). Smart Money buys here.
*   **Equal Highs/Lows (EQH / EQL)**: Major "magnets" for price. Often called "Retail Support/Resistance."
*   **Compression (LRLR - Low Resistance Liquidity Run)**: Trendline-like movement where price leaves liquidity on one side, leading to a fast "sweep" later.

### 2.2. Key Levels (Time-Based Liquidity)
*   **PDH / PDL**: Previous Day High / Low.
*   **PWH / PWL**: Previous Week High / Low.
*   **PMH / PML**: Previous Month High / Low.
*   **HOD / LOD**: High of Day / Low of Day (current).

## 3. Zones of Interest (POI - Point of Interest)
Zones where institutions are likely to react after a liquidity sweep.

### 3.1. Imbalance (FVG - Fair Value Gap)
*   **Definition**: A 3-candle gap where the wicks of candle 1 and candle 3 do not touch.
*   **Filling**: Price often returns to fill 50% (Equilibrium) or 100% (Full Fill) of the FVG.
*   **Invalidation**: Price closing beyond the FVG.

### 3.2. Order Block (OB)
*   **Definition**: The last candle before a strong impulsive move that breaks structure (BOS) and sweeps liquidity.
*   **Validation**: Must have a liquidity grab and a strong displacement (leaving FVG).
*   **Wick (Shadow)**: Sometimes the wick of the OB acts as the primary zone if the body is small.

### 3.3. Breaker & Mitigation Blocks
*   **Breaker Block (BB)**: A failed Order Block. An OB that was "broken" by price after a liquidity sweep and BOS. It becomes a support/resistance flip zone.
*   **Mitigation Block (MB)**: Similar to a Breaker, but it does NOT sweep liquidity before being broken. (Lower probability than BB).

## 4. Advanced Concepts & Timing

### 4.1. Killzones (London/NY Time)
*   **AO KZ (Asia)**: 00:00 - 05:00 UTC. Usually a range/accumulation.
*   **LO KZ (London Open)**: 07:00 - 10:00 UTC. Often creates the daily low/high (Manipulation).
*   **NYO KZ (New York Open)**: 12:00 - 15:00 UTC. Trend expansion or reversal (Distribution).
*   **LC KZ (London Close)**: 15:00 - 17:00 UTC. Often a retracement.

### 4.2. Power of Three (PO3)
The cycle of a daily candle:
1.  **Accumulation**: Range in Asia.
2.  **Manipulation**: Sweep of the range (Judas Swing) in London.
3.  **Distribution**: Trend expansion in NY.

### 4.3. Law of Effort (LoE)
*   **Momentum**: Fast movement = institutional effort.
*   **Correction**: Slow, choppy movement = lack of interest/liquidity seeking.
*   **Rule**: If a correction takes 22 candles but the impulse took 13, the trend is strong (Momentum favor).

## 5. Patterns & Execution Models

### 5.1. Three Drives Pattern (TDP)
A reversal pattern consisting of three consecutive higher highs (bearish) or lower lows (bullish) that test a POI.

### 5.2. Three Tap Setup (TTS)
Three tests of a zone. The 2nd tap often sweeps the 1st tap's liquidity, and the 3rd tap provides the entry.

### 5.3. Quasimodo Pattern (QML)
A high-probability reversal setup:
1.  Sweep of liquidity.
2.  BOS (Market Structure Shift).
3.  Return to the origin (OB/FVG) for entry.

### 5.4. Fibonacci OTE (Optimal Trade Entry)
*   **Levels**: 0.618, 0.705, 0.786.
*   **Usage**: Draw from Swing Low to Swing High. Look for entries in the OTE zone (Discount for Longs, Premium for Shorts).

## 6. Practical Trading Rules
1.  **HTF Priority**: 80% of trades should follow the HTF trend (1M -> 1W -> 1D -> 4H).
2.  **POI Refinement**: Find a zone on 1H/4H, wait for mBOS on 5m/15m.
3.  **8 Instruments Checklist**:
    *   Structure (HH/HL or LH/LL).
    *   Liquidity Sweep.
    *   BOS/MSS.
    *   FVG presence.
    *   Order Block / Breaker.
    *   Time (Killzone).
    *   Fibonacci (OTE/Premium/Discount).
